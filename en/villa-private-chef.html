In-Villa Dining

A villa stay should feel expansive, never organised around the kitchen.

The point of a villa is space: time opening up, the house settling into its own rhythm, and the people in it moving without pressure.

A private chef service should protect that feeling, whether it begins with an arrival dinner after travel, a late morning breakfast on the terrace, or an evening table that lingers long after sunset.

We keep the menus bespoke and the execution discreet, so the villa continues to feel like a place of ease rather than a schedule to be managed.

What's Included

Service rhythm shaped around the stay

The visible part is the table. Behind it sits menu planning, sourcing, kitchen adaptation, service flow, and clean execution inside the rhythm of the villa.

  • Menu planning for arrival dinners, breakfasts, lunches, sunset dinners, and lighter daytime service
  • Ingredient sourcing, prep, cooking, service, and post-service kitchen reset
  • Adaptation to the actual villa kitchen, layout, timing, and hosting style of the stay
  • Dietary planning for children, mixed guest groups, and allergy-aware requests
  • Coordination around check-ins, excursions, celebration nights, and the overall house schedule
Coverage Across the Stay

Formats that follow the rhythm of the villa

Arrival Dinner & First Evening

After travel, the first night usually needs ease more than theatre. An arrival dinner can make the house feel settled immediately without sending guests out to solve logistics after check-in.

Breakfast, Lunch & Poolside Service

Not every villa stay wants a formal dinner every day. Some groups need a softer daytime rhythm: breakfast setup, fruit and pastries, a relaxed lunch, or food that sits naturally around the terrace and pool.

Sunset Dinner & Celebration Night

When one evening needs more shape, the service can move into a slower, more expressive dinner format, whether that means plated courses, sharing menus, or a cuisine direction with a stronger point of view.

Longer Stays & Repeating Service

For longer bookings, we can build a service rhythm across several days instead of treating each meal as a one-off event. That keeps the hospitality consistent while allowing the menus to stay varied.

How We Work

Everything feels easier when the planning disappears into the stay

The finest villa hospitality is almost invisible in the moment. It is prepared carefully in advance, so the stay itself can remain slow, private, and beautifully unforced.

The process

01

Stay brief & villa context

We start with the dates, guest count, children, dietary needs, arrival pattern, and the practical setup of the villa kitchen.

02

Menu direction & service rhythm

We align which meals need service, which evenings need more ceremony, and how relaxed or formal the stay should feel from the guest side.

03

On-site execution

Once confirmed, sourcing, prep, service, and clean-up run quietly around the rhythm of the house instead of interrupting it.

Can you work in rented villa kitchens?

Yes. The service plan is adapted to the actual kitchen, storage, access, and timing realities of the property.

Can the service cover more than one day?

Yes. We can structure service across several days, from arrival dinner through breakfasts, lunches, and key evening tables.

Can menus adapt to children and dietary needs?

Yes. Mixed groups, children, vegetarian preferences, gluten-free requests, and allergy-aware planning are handled inside the menu design rather than as last-minute exceptions.

Planning a villa stay in Greece?

Share the dates, the villa, and the rhythm you want the stay to hold. We will return with a proposal shaped to the house, the group, and the mood of the trip.
